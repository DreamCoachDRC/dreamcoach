If you hear the pyramids of Egypt calling out to you or feel keen to uncover the mysteries of the Aztecs, this career of the week is just for you! Whip out those fedora hats and get your brushes out because it’s time to make Indiana Jones proud, with Archaeology.

The job of an archaeologist is to study past human activity by excavating, dating and interpreting objects and sites of historical interest. They implement excavation projects, informally known as digs, preserve archaeological remains and collect data that informs their understanding of the past. It demands skills like strong teamwork, focus, communication, patience, dedication, mastery in using various tools and instruments and an enthusiasm and zeal to know.

Pursuing Archeology is not limited by any particular stream or score and can be done by undertaking any major subjects offered in the curriculum.

Undergraduate degrees for Archaeology can be taken after completing standard 12th. These include Diploma in Indian Archaeology, Bachelor of Arts in Indian History Culture and Archaeology and Bachelor of Arts in Archaeology and Museology.

UNDERGRADUATE 

To start with, you’ll need to obtain an undergraduate degree or a diploma in the field of archaeology. Popular courses include Diploma in Indian Archaeology, Bachelor of Arts in Indian History Culture and Archaeology and Bachelor of Arts in Archaeology and Museology. If you have the zeal to dive further and gain more expertise in the field, you could pursue a masters degree in archaeology. You would have to choose between MA in Archaeology, MA in Ancient Indian History and Archaeology, Master of Science in Archaeology , Master of Philosophy in Ancient Indian History, Culture and Archaeology, Post-Graduate Diploma in Archaeology and Doctor of Philosophy in Ancient Indian History, Culture and Archaeology.

IN THE FIELD

In our country as an archaeologist that has just made it, you can expect a salary between Rs.2.5 to 3.5 lakhs on a yearly basis.After gaining experience in the field and sharpening your skills as a professional you can expect to earn between Rs.5 to 8 lakhs on a yearly basis.

Brajj Basi Lal, better known as B. B. Lal, is a renowned Indian archaeologist who was born on the 2nd of May 1921, in the Jhansi district located in the state of Uttar Pradesh. He specialised in the prehistoric archaeology of the Hindustan Peninsula.

After graduating from Allahabad University in 1943, he developed an interest in archaeology and became a trainee in excavation under a veteran British archaeologist, Mortimer Wheeler, starting with Taxila, and later at sites such as Harappa. He went on to become the director general of India’s archaeological survey in 1968 and served the office until 1972. He had directed the excavations that took place in Sisupalgarh (Sisulpargarh), Hastinapura, Birbhanpur, Gilund, and Kalibangan. He is one of the researchers of the Harappan civilization.

He also served on various UNESCO committees. After serving the office till the year 1972 he served as the director of the Indian Institute of Advanced Studies, Shimla.

But what if you decide you don't like Archeology and it's not what you wanted after completing your masters? Is it too late to change your mind? No its never too late. Don't worry, because you have a plethora of opportunities lying further ahead. Apart from being an archaeologist, you could inspect historic buildings / be a conservation officer, a museum/gallery curator or even a heritage manager! If you want to stay in the field of academics, you could be a lecturer, a documentation specialist or a museum education officer.

If you yearn for big bucks, you’ll have to look out for the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI), National heritage agencies and the Indian Council of Historical Research (ICHR) while trying to get yourself recruited.
